GST Calculator

🇮🇳 India Specific

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) calculator helps Indian businesses and consumers quickly calculate tax amounts across all four GST slabs — 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Whether you need to add GST to an ex-GST price or extract GST from an inclusive price, this free tool gives you the exact CGST, SGST, and total amounts in seconds.

How to Use

  1. Enter the amount in rupees. Use the price before GST for Add GST, or the invoice price for Remove GST.
  2. Select the GST slab that applies to your product or service.
  3. Choose Add GST for exclusive pricing or Remove GST for inclusive pricing.
  4. Select Calculate GST to view base amount, CGST, SGST, and final total.
  5. Use the chart to compare the taxable base against the GST amount visually.

GST Formula

Exclusive: GST Amount = (Base Price × GST Rate) / 100
Inclusive: Base Price = Total / (1 + GST Rate/100)
CGST = SGST = Total GST / 2

For an exclusive price, GST is added on top of the base amount. Multiply the base price by the GST rate and divide by 100 to get the tax. For an inclusive price, the GST is already inside the total, so divide the total by one plus the rate as a decimal. In most intra-state sales, total GST is split equally between Central GST and State GST. For inter-state sales, IGST usually applies instead of CGST and SGST.

Worked Example

A freelancer charges ₹50,000 for a service. GST rate is 18%.

GST Amount = ₹50,000 × 18 / 100 = ₹9,000. CGST = ₹4,500 and SGST = ₹4,500.

Invoice total = ₹50,000 + ₹9,000 = ₹59,000.

FAQ

What is GST in India?

GST is Goods and Services Tax, an indirect tax charged on the supply of many goods and services in India. It replaced several earlier indirect taxes and created a more unified tax structure. Businesses collect GST from customers and deposit it with the government after adjusting eligible input tax credit.

What are the 4 GST slabs?

The four common GST slabs are 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Some essential goods are taxed at 0%, while a few luxury or sin goods may also attract compensation cess. The correct slab depends on the product or service classification under GST rules.

What is the difference between CGST and SGST?

CGST is Central Goods and Services Tax and SGST is State Goods and Services Tax. For sales within the same state, the total GST is usually divided equally between CGST and SGST. For sales between states, IGST applies and is collected as one integrated tax.

How is GST calculated on a product?

If the price is exclusive of GST, multiply the base price by the GST rate and divide by 100. Add that GST amount to the base price for the final invoice total. If the price already includes GST, reverse the calculation by dividing the total by one plus the rate divided by 100.

Who needs to pay GST in India?

Registered businesses collect GST from buyers and pay it to the government. Registration rules depend on turnover, business type, state, and whether supplies are made across state borders or through e-commerce operators. Consumers effectively bear GST as part of the price paid for taxable goods and services.
